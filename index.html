🛂 Seychelles Travel Authorisation (TA)

UK citizens do not need a visa. You need a Travel Authorisation — a free online form for each traveller, submitted within 30 days of arrival.

Apply here: seychelles.govtas.com

📋 What you need to apply (per person):

  • Valid passport — must cover the full duration of stay
  • Return flight booking — confirmation for EK708 on 14th July
  • Accommodation booking — DoubleTree Hilton confirmation
  • Travel & health insurance — must be valid for Seychelles dates (required)
  • Passport-style photo — digital copy, clear and recent
  • Email address — approval is sent by email

⏱️ Timeline:

  • Apply within 30 days of arrival — earliest date to apply: 5th June 2026
  • Approval typically within 3–5 days
  • Apply for both travellers — one form each
  • Free of charge
  • Print or save the approval email — may be checked at boarding

Also available via the Seychelles e-Border app on iOS and Android. Source: Official TA guide

💉 Vaccinations

No mandatory vaccinations for UK travellers flying via Dubai. Yellow fever vaccination is only required if arriving from or transiting through an endemic country — neither the UK nor UAE are endemic.

Recommended by NHS / NaTHNaC:

  • Routine UK vaccinations up to date — check with GP
  • Hepatitis A — recommended for Seychelles. Single dose, effective within 2 weeks. Book at least 2 weeks before travel (by 19th June)
  • Tetanus — booster if last dose was more than 10 years ago
  • Hepatitis B — consider if not already vaccinated
  • Typhoid — consider, especially if eating outside hotel

Action:

  • Book a travel health appointment with GP or pharmacy 6–8 weeks before travel (mid-May)
  • Some vaccines need time to take effect — don't leave it late
  • No malaria risk in Seychelles — no antimalarials needed

Source: NaTHNaC — Seychelles · NHS Fit for Travel

🏨 Hilton Garden Inn — Heathrow T2/T3 (3rd–4th July)

Connected to Terminal 2 via covered walkway and Terminal 3 via pedestrian underpass. Easy access for Emirates T3 departure on 4th July.

Facilities:

  • 369 air-conditioned rooms with free Wi-Fi, safe, tea/coffee
  • 24-hour front desk
  • Fitness centre
  • Runway Bar — rooftop with runway views

Evening Meal — 3rd July:

  • The Apron Restaurant (on-site) — dinner 5:30pm–10:30pm. Pizzas, curries, rib-eye steaks, burgers, chicken wings, gyozas. Relaxed, modern setting. Easiest option after travel.
  • The Apron Bar (on-site) — 5pm–10:30pm. Lighter options: paninis, wraps, burgers, drinks.
  • The Runway Bar (on-site) — cocktails and drinks with runway views. Good for a pre-dinner drink.

Recommendation: Eat at The Apron on-site — no transfer needed, good menu, and you'll arrive around 6pm after travel from Swansea. Keep it simple.

🚶 Getting to Emirates Check-in (T3) on 4th July:

  1. Leave hotel via the covered walkway bridge into the Terminal 2 car park
  2. Enter Terminal 2 and follow signs for Terminal 3
  3. Take the lift or escalator down to the pedestrian underpass
  4. Follow the underpass — it leads directly to Terminal 3 departures
  5. Emirates check-in desks are in the Terminal 3 check-in hall
Allow 20–30 minutes with luggage and reduced mobility. The underpass is flat but it's a long walk. Consider requesting a wheelchair or buggy at the T2 information desk for transfer to T3. Alternatively, ask the hotel to arrange a drop-off at T3 departures.

💳 Banking & Cards

Both Monzo and Chase are excellent travel cards. Take both and keep one as backup.

Chase UK (recommended as primary):

  • No foreign transaction fees — Mastercard exchange rate, no markup
  • No ATM fees abroad — up to £500/day and £1,500/month
  • Local ATM operators may charge their own fee (Chase won't reimburse these)
  • Always pay in local currency (SCR) — never accept GBP conversion at the till

Monzo:

  • No fees on card spending abroad — Mastercard exchange rate
  • ATM withdrawals: first £200 free per 30 days outside EEA, then 2.5% fee
  • Good as a backup card or for smaller purchases
  • Instant spending notifications useful for tracking holiday budget

Before you go:

  • Notify both banks you're travelling to Seychelles (3rd–15th July) and Dubai (transit) — prevents cards being blocked
  • Check daily/monthly ATM limits are sufficient
  • Ensure PINs are memorised (chip and PIN used in Seychelles)
  • Cards widely accepted at hotels and restaurants — smaller shops and taxis may be cash only
  • ATMs available at Mahé airport and in Victoria

Sources: Chase UK — Travel · Monzo — Fees abroad

📱 Mobile Data & Coverage (O2)

Seychelles is in O2's "Rest of World" zone. Standard roaming is extremely expensive — up to £7.20 per MB for data. You need a plan or alternative.

Option 1: O2 Travel Bolt On (easiest):

  • £7.50 per day — only charged on days you connect to a network
  • Includes 500 mins, 500 texts, and 1GB data per day
  • Add via My O2 app before you travel
  • Works for both phones — add to each O2 account separately
  • For 10 days on holiday: approx. £75 per phone (£150 total)
  • Check Seychelles is included in the bolt-on destination list — use O2's travel search tool to confirm

Option 2: Travel eSIM (cheaper, recommended):

  • Buy a Seychelles data eSIM before you go — installs alongside your O2 SIM
  • Providers: Airalo, Saily, Holafly — from approx. £6–15 for 1–5GB over 7–30 days
  • Data only — use Wi-Fi calling or WhatsApp for calls
  • Check your phone supports eSIM (most iPhones from XS onwards, most Samsung S20+)
  • Buy and install before you leave the UK — activate on arrival
  • Much cheaper than O2 roaming for data-heavy use

Option 3: Hotel Wi-Fi only (free):

  • DoubleTree has free Wi-Fi — fine for hotel days
  • No data when out and about (taxis, Victoria, beaches)
  • Download offline maps (Google Maps) before you go as backup

Before you go:

  • Turn off data roaming on both phones before leaving the UK (re-enable only if you have a bolt-on or eSIM active)
  • Download offline Google Maps for Mahé
  • Download any entertainment (Netflix, Kindle) on hotel Wi-Fi
  • Network coverage on Mahé is generally good in tourist areas (Airtel and Cable & Wireless)

Sources: O2 — Using phone abroad · Best eSIM for Seychelles
